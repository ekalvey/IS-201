Team Information

The Oakland Athletics (A’s) are a professional Major League Baseball team with a storied history, originally founded in 1901 as the Philadelphia Athletics. Known for their iconic green and gold colors and innovative strategies, the team has won multiple World Series championships. The A’s currently play in Oakland but are planning a move to Las Vegas, where they will play in a new state-of-the-art stadium, continuing their legacy and fan experience in a new city.

  1. General Info:
    • Location: Oakland, California (moving to Las Vegas, Nevada)
    • League: Major League Baseball (MLB)
    • Team Colors: Green, Gold, White
  2. Stadium:
    • Current: RingCentral Coliseum, Oakland
    • Future: New Stadium in Las Vegas (under construction)
    • Capacity: Approx. 30,000 (future stadium)

Team History

The Oakland Athletics were originally founded in 1901 as the Philadelphia Athletics, one of the American League's charter teams. They moved to Kansas City in 1955 and became the Kansas City Athletics, before relocating to Oakland in 1968. Over the years, the A's have won 9 World Series championships and have been known for their innovative strategies, including the use of analytics in player development. The team has a rich legacy of developing talent and cultivating a strong fan base in the Bay Area. Currently, the Athletics are preparing to move to Las Vegas where a new state-of-the-art stadium will continue the team’s storied tradition in a new city.
